My first blog post

Hello citizens of the internet! For my first post, I guess I should introduce myself. I'm Arnav, a freshman at Purdue University studying cybersecurity. I decided to start this blog after a meeting with a few members of the Anvaya Solutions team, a cybersecurity company based in my hometown of Folsom, California. One of the people I talked with, Bek, recommended I start a blog to document my learning and personal projects, so here we are.

You might be wondering why this website looks so good, and it's all intentional. I thought I'd go with a more minimalist approach, as I actually kind of feel like this makes my page stand out from every other generic bootstrap html blog clone. And who doesn't love monospaced fonts?? As of writing this post, I only have a barebones template for myself to work off of and a few page templates for me to use. I haven't even hosted this yet, but I'm thinking either Github pages or Heroku. I also plan on adding a section to display personal projects in the near future, but for nowhere's a linkto my old portfolio website so you can see how much better this one is.

Right now, relevant classes I'm taking are:
CNIT270 - Cybersecurity Fundamentals
CNIT15501 - Introduction to Sofware Development Concepts
As of now, I haven't learned anything notable or new but I'll update this as I go along.

I'm also part of a development team working on a grant application for the Purdue Office of Engagement through the Data Mine at Purdue, a data science focused learning community. My main role is on the backend team, which involves writing APIs and working on the database schema. The project is built with React and Express.js and we are using TypeScript for the entirety of it, which has been interesting to use. I will say I like it more than vanilla JS, but not enough to actually use it on my own 😬. I'm not the biggest JavaScript fan.

In terms of my self-learning right now, I've been getting into learning C. It's something I have basically no experience in, as I've just been using relatively higher level languages in the past (Python, JS, etc). I feel like my understanding of pretty fundamental software development concepts such as pointers and memory allocation is lacking, and I think learning C will give me some insight into that. Also, C just seems like a really important language to know in general if I want to get into reverse engineering and writing some of my own exploits. For now, the most I've done is a basic calculator 🙄 but hopefully I'll have some more interesting stuff to show on here soon.

I think I'll also make a post going over my terminal workflow and some apps I use because I think that'll be fun to write about, but this is it for now. Thanks for reading!
